Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026 Last Updated: January 1, 2026

1. Who This Privacy Policy Applies To

This Privacy Policy applies to Zynix AI, Inc. and its subsidiaries (collectively, "Zynix," "we," "us," or "our"). It governs how we collect, use, disclose, and otherwise process personal information in connection with our:

  • Websites (including zynix.ai)
  • Mobile applications
  • Products and services
  • Customer relationships
  • Marketing communications

This policy does not apply to patient health information processed on behalf of our healthcare customer clients as Business Associates under HIPAA. Such information is governed by Business Associate Agreements (BAAs).

2. Information We Collect

Information You Provide Directly

When you interact with us, we may collect:

  • Account Information: Name, email address, password, organization, job title, phone number
  • Contact Information: Email, mailing address, telephone number
  • Billing Information: Payment method, billing address, invoicing details
  • Content You Create: Messages, feedback, support tickets, survey responses
  • Preferences: Communication preferences, account settings

Information Collected Automatically

When you use our services, we automatically collect:

  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, browser type
  • Usage Information: Pages visited, features used, time spent, interaction patterns
  • Location Information: IP address, approximate geographic location (country, state)
  • Cookies & Tracking: Via cookies, pixels, and similar technologies for analytics and personalization
  • Performance Data: Error logs, system diagnostics, performance metrics

Information Collected on Behalf of Customers (HIPAA)

When acting as a Business Associate for healthcare customers, we may process Protected Health Information (PHI) including:

  • Patient names, medical record numbers, dates of service
  • Clinical data, diagnoses, treatment plans, medication information
  • Lab results, imaging notes, clinical documentation
  • Insurance information and payment details

Important: PHI is processed strictly in accordance with HIPAA regulations and our Business Associate Agreements with each customer. We do not use or disclose PHI except as authorized by the BAA or as required by law.

Information from Third Parties

We may collect information about you from:

  • Business partners and vendors
  • Marketing partners for analytics and lead generation
  • Public databases and records
  • Other services you authorize to share information with us

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for purposes including:

  • Service Delivery: Providing, maintaining, and improving our products and services
  • Account Management: Creating and managing your account, authentication, access control
  • Communication: Responding to inquiries, sending service updates, technical notifications
  • Billing & Payments: Processing transactions, billing, and payment collection
  • Analytics: Understanding usage patterns, improving user experience, product development
  • Marketing: Sending promotional content, announcements about new features (with your consent)
  • Security: Detecting fraud, preventing abuse, protecting against malicious activity
  • Legal Compliance: Complying with legal obligations, responding to lawful requests
  • Customer Support: Resolving issues, troubleshooting, technical assistance

Legal Basis: We process information based on: contract performance, your consent, our legitimate interests, legal obligations, or protection of vital interests.

4. How We Share Your Information

We may share information in these circumstances:

With Service Providers

We share information with vendors who provide services on our behalf including cloud hosting, analytics, payment processing, customer support, and marketing. All service providers are contractually bound to protect your information and use it only as necessary to provide services.

With Business Partners

We may share aggregated or anonymized information with business partners for marketing, research, or business development purposes.

For Legal Reasons

We may disclose information when required by law, regulation, legal process (subpoena, court order), or government request. We will provide notice when legally permissible.

Business Transfers

If Zynix is acquired, merges, files bankruptcy, or transfers assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you of material changes.

With Your Consent

We may share information when you explicitly consent or direct us to do so.

We Do Not Sell Personal Information: Zynix does not sell, trade, or rent personal information to third parties for their marketing purposes. We respect your privacy.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies, pixels, web beacons, and similar technologies for:

  • Essential Cookies: Authentication, security, session management
  • Analytics: Understanding how you use our services (Google Analytics)
  • Personalization: Customizing content and recommendations
  • Marketing: Tracking campaigns, retargeting advertisements

Most browsers allow you to control or delete cookies. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Disabling cookies may affect service functionality.

Do Not Track: We honor "Do Not Track" browser signals where applicable.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information for the period necessary to provide services and fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

  • Account Information: Retained while your account is active, plus 30 days after closure
  • Billing Records: Retained for 7 years (tax/legal requirements)
  • Support Tickets: Retained for 2 years
  • Analytics Data: Aggregated data retained indefinitely; individual-level data deleted after 12 months
  • PHI (HIPAA): Retained per customer BAA specifications and HIPAA requirements

You may request deletion of your personal information by contacting us, except where we are required by law to retain it or where it is necessary to provide services.

7. Data Security

We implement comprehensive security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Secure authentication with multi-factor authentication (MFA) for sensitive accounts
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Access controls with role-based permissions
  • Monitoring and intrusion detection
  • Business continuity and disaster recovery plans
  • Employee security training and background checks

No Absolute Security: While we employ industry-standard security measures, no transmission or storage method is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. Your Privacy Rights

General Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal information
  • Correction: Update or correct inaccurate information
  • Deletion: Request deletion (subject to legal obligations)
  • Portability: Obtain your information in portable format
  • Opt-Out: Opt out of marketing communications

California Residents (CCPA)

California residents have rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act, including the right to know, delete, opt-out of sale, and non-discrimination for exercising rights.

European Residents (GDPR)

EU residents have rights including access, rectification, erasure, portability, objection to processing, and withdrawal of consent.

Exercising Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at info@zynix.ai with "Privacy Request" in the subject line. We will respond within the timeframe required by applicable law (typically 30 days).

9. International Data Transfers

Zynix is based in the United States. Information we collect may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in the United States or other countries where we operate. These countries may have data protection laws different from your home country.

When we transfer data internationally, we implement safeguards including:

  • Standard contractual clauses for EU to US transfers
  • Privacy Shield frameworks (where applicable)
  • Your explicit consent to transfer

By using our services, you consent to transfer of your information to countries outside your home country.

10. Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that a child has provided information, we will delete it promptly. Parents who believe their child's information has been collected may contact us.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify you of material changes by posting the updated policy with a new "Last Updated" date. Your continued use of our services after changes become effective constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
